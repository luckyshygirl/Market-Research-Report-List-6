<p><ul><li>Ultrasonic Additive Manufacturing (UAM)</li><li>Laminated Object Manufacturing (LOM)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Sheet lamination is a process used in additive manufacturing to create solid objects by layering sheets of material on top of each other. Ultrasonic Additive Manufacturing (UAM) uses ultrasonic vibrations to bond layers of metal together, producing high-quality parts with minimal heat input. Laminated Object Manufacturing (LOM) involves cutting and stacking layers of paper, plastic, or metal to create a prototype or final product. Both UAM and LOM offer unique advantages in terms of material flexibility, cost-effectiveness, and speed of production.</p></p>
