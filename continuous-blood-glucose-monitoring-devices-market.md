<p><p>Continuous blood glucose monitoring devices are divided into two main types: wearable and implantable. Wearable devices are typically worn externally on the body and continuously track blood glucose levels throughout the day. These devices are convenient and easy to use for patients. Implantable devices, on the other hand, are surgically implanted under the skin and provide constant monitoring of glucose levels. This type of device offers the advantage of being discreet and not requiring frequent calibration or replacement. Both types of devices play a crucial role in managing diabetes effectively.</p></p>
