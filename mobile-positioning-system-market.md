<p><ul><li>TOA</li><li>E-OTD</li><li>A-GPS</li></ul></p>
<p><p>TOA (Time of Arrival), E-OTD (Enhanced Observed Time Difference), and A-GPS (Assisted Global Positioning System) are all types of mobile positioning systems. TOA relies on measuring the time taken for signals to travel between a mobile device and multiple base stations. E-OTD works by comparing the timing of signals received at different base stations. A-GPS combines GPS technology with data from cellular networks to provide more accurate and faster location information. These systems are essential for accurate location tracking and navigation on mobile devices.</p></p>
