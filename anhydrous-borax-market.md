<p><p>The anhydrous borax market primarily consists of two types: burning borax and fusing hydrated borax. Burning borax involves the dehydration of borax at high temperatures to produce anhydrous borax, widely used in glassmaking and ceramics. Fusing hydrated borax, on the other hand, refers to the process of melting hydrated borax, which enhances its properties for applications like metallurgy and agricultural products. Both types cater to specific industrial demands, driven by unique physical and chemical properties.</p></p>
