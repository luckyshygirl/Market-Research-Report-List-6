<p><p>The automotive keyless fob market is primarily categorized into two types: Passive Keyless Entry Systems (PKES) and Remote Keyless Entry Systems (RKES). PKES allows users to unlock and start their vehicles automatically when the fob is in proximity, enhancing convenience and security. In contrast, RKES requires the user to press a button on the fob to unlock or start the vehicle, offering a traditional yet efficient method of access. Both systems aim to improve user experience and vehicle security.</p></p>
