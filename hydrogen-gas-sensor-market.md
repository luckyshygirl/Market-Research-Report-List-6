<p><p>The hydrogen gas sensor market is categorized into several types, including electrochemical, metal oxide semiconductor (MOS), catalytic, and others. Electrochemical sensors utilize chemical reactions to detect hydrogen levels, offering high sensitivity. Metal oxide semiconductor sensors rely on the change in electrical resistance, providing rapid response times. Catalytic sensors detect hydrogen through combustion, ensuring reliability in explosive environments. Other types may include optical and thermal sensors, each catering to specific applications and environments in industries such as automotive, aerospace, and safety.</p></p>
