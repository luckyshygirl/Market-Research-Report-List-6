<p><p>The presbyopia correction device market includes various types designed to address age-related vision decline. Corneal inlays are small, disk-like devices implanted in the cornea to improve near vision while maintaining distance vision. Scleral implants are placed on the sclera, utilizing a larger surface area to enhance focus for reading. Other devices include multifocal contact lenses and surgical options. Each type offers distinct advantages, catering to different patient needs and preferences for correcting presbyopia.</p></p>
