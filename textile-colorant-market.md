<p><p>The textile colorant market comprises several types, including acid colorants, alkaline colorants, and others. Acid colorants are typically used for dyeing proteins and nylon, offering vibrant hues and good lightfastness. Alkaline colorants, on the other hand, are suited for cellulosic fibers and provide stability under basic conditions. Other colorants include disperse dyes for synthetic fibers and reactive dyes for cotton, each tailored for specific applications, ensuring versatility in achieving desired coloration and fabric properties across various textiles.</p></p>
