<p><p>Plastic bonded hard ferrite magnets are manufactured using various processes, primarily injection molding and compression molding. Injection molding involves melting a plastic binder mixed with ferrite powder, allowing the material to be shaped into desired forms under high pressure and heat. Compression molding, on the other hand, involves pressing the mixture into molds at lower temperatures, allowing for simpler shapes but potentially more significant material density. Both methods cater to different applications based on performance, cost, and manufacturing efficiency.</p></p>
