<p><p>Composite insulators are electrical insulators made from a combination of materials, typically comprising a polymer core wrapped in a fiberglass sheath. They are designed to replace traditional porcelain and glass insulators due to their lightweight, high mechanical strength, and superior electrical performance. Composite insulators exhibit excellent resistance to weather conditions, pollution, and UV radiation, making them ideal for high-voltage transmission and distribution applications.</p><p>The Composite Insulators Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 6.40% during the forecast period. Factors contributing to this growth include the increasing demand for renewable energy sources and the necessity for modernized grid infrastructure. Additionally, the ongoing replacement of older insulator technologies with composite solutions in various applications, such as overhead power lines and substations, is driving market expansion.</p><p>Recent trends include innovations in material science, leading to the development of advanced composite materials that offer improved performance and longevity. Moreover, the rising emphasis on reducing the environmental impact of electrical infrastructure is encouraging the adoption of composite insulators, which generally have a lower carbon footprint compared to traditional insulators.
