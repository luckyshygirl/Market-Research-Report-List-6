<p><p>The blood storage devices market includes refrigerators and freezers specifically designed for preserving blood and blood components. Refrigerators maintain temperatures between 2°C to 6°C, ideal for storing whole blood and red cells. Freezers operate at lower temperatures, often at -20°C or colder, suitable for storing plasma and other blood components that require long-term preservation. Both types of devices ensure optimal conditions to maintain the integrity and viability of blood products, critical for transfusions and medical procedures.</p></p>
