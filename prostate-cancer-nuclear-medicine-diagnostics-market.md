<p><p>The prostate cancer nuclear medicine diagnostics market primarily involves two imaging techniques: Single Photon Emission Computed Tomography (SPECT) and Positron Emission Tomography (PET). SPECT utilizes gamma-ray emissions to create detailed three-dimensional images of prostate tumors, helping to assess their size and location. PET, on the other hand, detects metabolic activity, allowing for early detection of cancerous changes. Both methods enhance the accuracy of prostate cancer diagnosis and treatment planning, contributing to improved patient outcomes in clinical settings.</p></p>
