<p><p>Inorganic metal finishing encompasses various types of processes such as anodizing, cladding, conversion coatings, electroplating, electroless plating, and other specialized methods. Anodizing involves creating an oxide layer on the metal surface for increased corrosion resistance. Cladding involves applying a different metal layer onto the substrate for improved properties. Conversion coatings convert the metal surface into a corrosion-resistant compound. Electroplating involves depositing a thin layer of metal onto the substrate for improved appearance and durability. Electroless plating is a chemical process to deposit metal without using electricity. Other specialized methods may include powder coating, chemical polishing, and passivation.</p></p>
