<p><p>Epidemic insurance can be categorized into two main market types: life insurance and non-life insurance. Life insurance addresses scenarios where epidemics lead to increased mortality, providing financial support to beneficiaries upon the policyholder's death. In contrast, non-life insurance covers losses associated with business interruptions, healthcare costs, or property damage resulting from an epidemic. Both markets aim to mitigate financial risks linked to health crises, offering individuals and businesses a safety net during unforeseen public health emergencies.</p></p>
