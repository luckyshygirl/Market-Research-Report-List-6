<p><p>An Ignition Interlock Device (IID) is a breathalyzer installed in vehicles to prevent intoxicated individuals from starting the engine. The IID market is primarily divided into two technological types: fuel cell technology and semiconductor technology. Fuel cell IIDs use electrochemical reactions to accurately measure blood alcohol content, offering high precision and reliability. In contrast, semiconductor IIDs utilize sensor technology that is often more compact and cost-effective, but may provide slightly less accuracy. Both technologies serve the critical role of enhancing road safety.</p></p>
