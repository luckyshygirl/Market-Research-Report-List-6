<p><p>Crystalline silicon photovoltaic (PV) cells dominate the solar market, primarily categorized into monocrystalline silicon (Mono-Si) and multicrystalline silicon (Multi-Si) modules. Mono-Si modules, made from a single crystal structure, offer higher efficiency and a smaller footprint, making them ideal for space-constrained applications. In contrast, Multi-Si modules, constructed from multiple silicon crystals, are typically more cost-effective but have slightly lower efficiency. Both types are widely used in residential, commercial, and utility-scale solar installations, catering to diverse energy needs and budgets.</p></p>
